ABOUT

The website's goal is to convey the Boho interior design that is the studio's preferable and dominant style. The offer is oriented on people, who value elegant, clear and natural approach to materials and space and want to receive an individual, unique design tailored for them. The client visiting the website for the first time should understand immediately what is the style of the studio and what kind of service it has to offer. At the same time, the furniture renovation offer needs to be focused on, with an equal presence on the website. Moreover the designer wants to have a safe space where she could share her thoughts, trends, tips on interior design, that would be mainly dedicated for people that are not her clients (yet), with hope to attract new clients and movement on the website.

PROCESS

  1. General research about the interior design business.
  2. Analysing websites of other interior designers.
  3. Understanding what kind of service is provided by the studio.
  4. Understanding who would be the client and what they would look for on the website, how they could enter the website and how they could interact with it.
  5. Understanding what are the biggest challenges and goals of the web design.
  6. Creating personas.
  7. Creating user journey map.
  8. Creating framework of the website -> architecture of information.
  9. Low-fidelity ideating.
  10. Creating the style guide that will clearly represent studio design style.
  11. Creating wireframes and high-fidelity prototype.
  12. Conducting usability-tests.
  13. Adjusting the design based on the conclusions from the tests.

Personas

Photographer

Style

She has a very precise idea of what she would like her apartment to look like- Boho chic style. She has just bought a new apartment in the modern glass-facade building in the city centre. However, after a long, hard day at work, she would love to just relax and leave the busy city behind the apartment door and to enter the completely different realm of peace and quiet.

She would use the website to go through all the projects to decide whether or not she likes it. User journey

structure

How to convince more people to reach out and contact?

  • Contact information easily accessible: Contact tab, footer, link to contact form on offer page

How to acquire more traffic on the website ?

  • Blog with different articles about interior design.

How to make both services- furniture renovation and interior design equally important on the website ?

  • Both offer and projects tab in navigation menu are accessible for interior design and furniture renovation service.

Low-fidelity

How to communicate that Boho design studio is one-person business?

  • On the main page photo of the owner of Boho design studio.
  • On the studio page clearly written who is the woner, what does she do, her experience, once again her photo.

How to explain in a simple way the design process and different types of service available?

  • Design process divided into 4 steps, each step explained in details.
  • Types of service presented in a table with explaination how many, which drawings are included in every package.

Style guide

How to convey through website design very specific interior design style of the studio?

  • Natural, earthy colors
  • Rounded shapes, rounded frames of pictures
  • Serif font for main headers- elegance and style
  • Modern business - flat design

Usability tests

Using high-fidelity website's prototype I conducted usability tests on five individuals. My observations :

  • Offer page included both offer for interior design and for furniture renovation, because the last one was located down the page it was difficult tofind and some users didn't realise it was even there.
    > Clear separation of furniture renovation and interior design offer.
  • It was difficult for users to read paragraphs of light font color on the pinkish background- Colors of small contrast ratio.
    > Color of font consistent everywhere on the website - dark green, paired with background color - light beige - to give a contrast ratio of over 10 that meets WCAG standards.
  • Logo of the website was perceived as not modern and too complicated.
    > Logo redesign.
  • Users were looking for a contact information after seeing the offer page.
    > Link to contact form on the offer page.
  • Users thought that pictures on the main page illustrating the paragraphs were clickable elements.
    > Photos on the main page linked to the corresponding tab i.e. to interior design projects gallery.

Learnings

  • Sometimes users interact with the webiste not as expected. It is good to use this knowledge and provide them with interactive elements if that is going to imporove user flow.
  • Visual communication and design of the website was very importent in this project. Choice of the wrong colors, not contrasting enough or not associated with natural, boho style could have a real impact on the user's bahaviours and statistics of the website's traffic in general.
  • If more time the blog tab could have been created with more consideration.
  • Users who don't know the website and enter it for the first time, looking for some specific information, could not have the will to scroll down, search deeper in the structure of the website. If the specific section is highly visited or searched for it should be easily accessible from the main page or menu.
