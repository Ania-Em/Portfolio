ABOUT

The website's goal is to convey the boho interior design that is the studio's preferable and dominant style. The offer is oriented on people, who value elegant, clear and natural approach to materials, space and want to receive an individual, unique design tailored for them. The client visiting the website for the first time should understand immediately what is the style of the studio and what kind of service it has to offer. What is more, the studio has just launched new offer- furniture renovation, which also needs to be focused on, with an equal presence on the website. The studio owner wants to have a safe space where she could share her thoughts, trends, tips on interior design, that would be mainly dedicated for people that are not her clients (yet), with hope to attract new clients and movement on the website.

PROCESS

  1. General research about the interior design business.
  2. Analysing websites of other interior designers.
  3. Analysing services provided by the studio.
  4. Analysing the potential clients, their motives, their behaviour on the website.
  5. Biggest challenges and goals of the web design.
  6. Creating personas.
  7. Creating user journey map.
  8. Creating a framework of the website -> architecture of information.
  9. Low-fidelity ideating.
  10. Creating the style guide that will represent studio design style.
  11. Creating wireframes and high-fidelity prototype.
  12. Conducting usability-tests.
  13. Adjusting the design based on the conclusions from the tests.

Style guide

How to convey through website design very specific interior design style of the studio?

  • Natural, earthy colors
  • Rounded shapes, rounded frames of pictures
  • Serif font for main headers- elegance and style
  • Modern business - flat design

High-fidelity

How to convince more people to reach out and contact?

  • Contact information easily accessible: Contact tab, footer, link to contact form on offer page

How to make both services- furniture renovation and interior design equally important on the website ?

  • Both offer and projects tab in navigation menu are accessible for interior design and furniture renovation service.

How to acquire more traffic on the website ?

  • Blog with different articles about interior design.

How to communicate that Boho design studio is one-person business?

  • Photo of the owner of Boho design studio on the main page.
  • Clearly written who is the owner, what does she do, her experience, once again her photo on the studio page.

How to explain in a simple way the design process and different types of service available?

  • Design process divided into 4 steps, each step explained in details.
  • Types of service presented in a table with explaination how many, which drawings are included in every package.

Usability tests

Using a high-fidelity website's prototype I conducted usability tests on five individuals. My observations :

  • The offer page included both offers for interior design and furniture renovation, because the last one was located down the page it was difficult to find and some users didn't realise it was even there.
    > Clear separation of furniture renovation and interior design offer.
  • It was difficult for users to read paragraphs of light font color on the pinkish background- Colors of small contrast ratio.
    > Color of font consistent everywhere on the website - dark green, paired with background color - light beige - to give a contrast ratio of over 10 that meets WCAG standards.
  • The logo of the website was perceived as not modern and too complicated.
    > Logo redesign.
  • Users were looking for a contact information after seeing the offer page.
    > Link to contact form on the offer page.
  • Users thought that pictures on the main page illustrating the paragraphs were clickable elements.
    > Photos on the main page linked to the corresponding tab i.e. to interior design projects gallery.

Learnings

  • Sometimes users interact with the website not as expected. It is good to observe their first instinctive reactions and adjust the design to it, i.e. provide interactive elements if that is going to improve the user flow.
  • Visual communication and design of the website were very important in this project. The wrong choice of colours, not contrasting enough or not associated with natural, boho style, could have a real impact on the user's behaviours and statistics of the website's traffic.
  • If there was more time given the blog tab could have been created with more consideration.
  • Users who don't know the website and enter it for the first time, look for specific information, do not have the will to scroll down or to search deeper in the structure of the website. If the specific section is highly visited or searched for, it should be accessible from the main page or menu.
